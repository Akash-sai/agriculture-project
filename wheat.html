WHEAT:-
1.Soil Preparation:Choose a well-draining field with loamy soil. Prepare the soil by plowing and harrowing to create a fine seedbed. Ensure the soil pH is suitable for wheat cultivation.

2.Planting:Sow wheat seeds at the right time, typically in the fall for winter wheat or in the spring for spring wheat. Plant seeds at the recommended depth and spacing, covering them with soil.

3.Fertilization:Apply fertilizers based on soil tests and local recommendations. Nitrogen is particularly crucial for wheat, and the application timing depends on the wheat growth stages.

4.Weed and Pest Control:Monitor the field for weeds and pests. Use appropriate herbicides and pesticides to control unwanted vegetation and insects that may harm the wheat crop.

5.Harvesting:Harvest wheat when it reaches maturity and the moisture content is suitable for harvesting. This typically occurs in late spring or early summer, depending on the wheat variety and planting time.
