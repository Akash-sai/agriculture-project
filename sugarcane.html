SUGAR CANE:


1.Land Preparation:Choose a well-drained field with fertile soil. Plow and harrow the soil to break it up and create a suitable seedbed. Sugarcane prefers slightly acidic to neutral soil.

2.Planting:Obtain healthy sugarcane stalks or setts from a reliable source. Plant these setts in furrows or trenches, burying them at a depth of 2-4 inches. Maintain proper spacing between the setts.

3.Irrigation:Ensure consistent and adequate irrigation, especially during dry periods. Sugarcane requires a significant amount of water for optimal growth.

4.Weed Control and Fertilization:Control weeds regularly, as they can compete with sugarcane for nutrients and water. Apply fertilizers with a balance of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium to support healthy growth.

5.Harvesting:Sugarcane is typically ready for harvest about 12-18 months after planting. Harvest when the crop is mature and the sugar content is at its peak. Cut the canes close to the ground using a machete or similar tool.
