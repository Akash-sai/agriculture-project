TOMATO:-


1.Seed Starting or Seedling Purchase:Begin by either starting tomato seeds indoors several weeks before the last expected frost or purchasing young tomato seedlings. Ensure seeds or seedlings are of a suitable tomato variety for your climate.

2.Planting:Choose a sunny location with well-draining soil. Transplant seedlings or plant seeds outdoors after the danger of frost has passed. Space the plants according to the specific recommendations for the tomato variety.

3.Watering and Fertilization:Water consistently to keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. Fertilize with a balanced fertilizer or one specifically formulated for tomatoes. Follow recommended guidelines for application rates and timing.

4.Support and Pruning:Provide support for tomato plants using stakes, cages, or trellises to keep the fruit off the ground. Prune the plants by removing suckers (side shoots) to encourage better air circulation and fruit production.

5.Harvesting:Harvest tomatoes when they reach the desired size and color. Tomatoes are typically ready for harvest when they have a firm, fully colored appearance. Gently twist or cut the fruit from the vine.
